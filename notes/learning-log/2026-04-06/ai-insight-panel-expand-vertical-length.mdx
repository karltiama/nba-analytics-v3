## Context
The betting right-rail AI panel felt too short vertically, reducing visible summary context and making the sidebar feel cramped.

## Goal
Expand the AI sidebar panel to a stronger vertical footprint while preserving scroll-safe behavior.

## Files Changed
- `components/betting/AIInsightPanel.tsx`

## Implementation Notes (key logic explained)
- Updated the outer panel height constraints:
  - Added `min-h-[70vh]` so the panel occupies substantial vertical space.
  - Increased max height from `max-h-[calc(100vh-8rem)]` to `max-h-[calc(100vh-5rem)]`.
- Kept overflow handling unchanged so content remains scrollable inside the panel.

## Key Decisions (and alternatives considered)
- Chose CSS constraint adjustment only (smallest safe diff).
- Alternative considered: changing sticky container offsets/padding in page layout; deferred to avoid broader layout side effects.

## Assumptions
- Primary target is desktop/right-rail experience on `/betting`.

## Failure Modes
- On short viewports, panel may still feel tight due to surrounding sticky spacing.
- If future header height changes, `calc(100vh-5rem)` may need retuning.

## How to Test (commands + steps)
1. Open `/betting` on desktop width (`xl` breakpoint or wider).
2. Confirm sidebar panel appears taller than before.
3. Confirm summary and footer remain visible/usable with internal scrolling.
4. Resize viewport height and ensure no overflow outside viewport.

## Next Steps
- If you want even taller, try `min-h-[75vh]` or reduce sticky wrapper padding in `app/betting/page.tsx`.
