## Context
Even after combo-stat mapping fixes, the explorer still produced too many rows with EV > 30%, indicating model probabilities could diverge too far from market-implied probabilities.

## Goal
Add a market-anchor guardrail to keep model probabilities within a bounded distance of market implied probability and reduce unrealistic EV outliers.

## Files Changed
- `lib/betting/player-prop-ev-row.ts`

## Implementation Notes (key logic explained)
- Added `marketProb = 1 / decimalOdds` (clamped to `[0, 1]`).
- Added guardrail:
  - `MAX_MODEL_MARKET_PROB_GAP = 0.2`
  - `anchorToMarket(modelProb, marketProb)` clamps model probability to `marketProb ± 0.2`.
- Applied anchoring to:
  - baseline (`pBase`)
  - Track A calibrated probability (`pTrackA`)
  - Track B calibrated probability (`pTrackB`)

## Key Decisions (and alternatives considered)
- Decision: probability anchoring instead of direct EV clipping.
- Alternative: hard EV max cap.
  - Rejected because EV clipping hides, rather than controls, confidence divergence.

## Assumptions
- Market implied probability is a useful anchor for first-pass plausibility constraints.
- A ±0.2 envelope balances exploration with risk control.

## Failure Modes
- True mispricing opportunities with larger model-market gaps may be compressed.
- If market probabilities are stale/poor quality, anchor can over-constrain.

## How to Test (commands + steps)
1. `npm run build`
2. Query explorer:
   - `/api/betting/props-explorer?date=<today>&sort=ev&dir=desc&limit=200`
3. Compare counts of `ev > 0.30` before vs after.
4. Spot-check top rows to ensure projections and EV look plausible.

## Next Steps
- Tune `MAX_MODEL_MARKET_PROB_GAP` by backtest (`0.15`, `0.2`, `0.25`).
- Refit calibration after settling the guardrail.
