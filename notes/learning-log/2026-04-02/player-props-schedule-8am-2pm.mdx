## Context
The player props ingestion scheduler was running every 15 minutes, which felt excessive and likely increased database load unnecessarily.

## Goal
Reduce schedule frequency to two daily runs at 8:00 AM ET and 2:00 PM ET.

## Files Changed
- `infra/terraform.tfvars`

## Implementation Notes (key logic explained)
- Replaced high-frequency cron list with two explicit cron expressions.
- Kept `player_props_schedule_timezone` as `America/New_York` so run times are interpreted in ET.
- Added:
  - `cron(0 8 ? * * *)` for 8:00 AM ET
  - `cron(0 14 ? * * *)` for 2:00 PM ET

## Key Decisions (and alternatives considered)
- Decision: use two explicit cron entries rather than a `rate(...)` expression.
  - Why: exact, predictable execution windows.
  - Alternative: `rate(6 hours)` (rejected because it does not align to fixed business times).

## Assumptions
- EventBridge Scheduler resources are managed via Terraform in this repo.
- `player_props_schedule_crons` overrides `player_props_schedule_expression` when non-empty.

## Failure Modes
- If Terraform apply is not run, AWS schedule remains unchanged.
- If timezone is changed accidentally, runs could fire at unintended local times.
- If cron syntax is edited incorrectly, scheduler creation/update can fail.

## How to Test (commands + steps)
1. Apply infra changes:
   - `cd infra`
   - `terraform plan`
   - `terraform apply`
2. Verify deployed schedules:
   - `aws scheduler list-schedules --max-results 100 --query "Schedules[?contains(Name, 'player-props')].[Name,State,ScheduleExpression,ScheduleExpressionTimezone]" --output table`
3. Confirm two schedules exist for player props at 8 and 14 ET.
4. Check controller Lambda invocations around those times in CloudWatch.
