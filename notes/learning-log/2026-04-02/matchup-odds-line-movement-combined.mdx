## Context
The betting matchup page showed Odds and Line movement as two separate glass cards with `space-y-4`, which felt sparse.

## Goal
Tighten UX by combining odds and line movement into one section with less vertical whitespace.

## Files Changed
- `components/betting/MatchupPageLayout.tsx`

## Implementation Notes (key logic explained)
- Single `glass-card` for `section-odds` with title **Odds & line movement**.
- **Current odds** lives in a subtle inner panel (`border`, `bg-white/[0.02]`) with slightly reduced typography on small screens.
- **Movement** sits below a divider (`border-t`) with a small label, then the existing spread/total tabs and `LineMovementChart`.
- Chart `height` reduced from 200 → 176 to keep the combined block shorter.
- Sticky nav label for this anchor updated to **Odds & lines** (`SECTION_LABELS`).

## Key Decisions (and alternatives considered)
- Decision: one card, stacked layout (odds then charts).
  - Alternative: side-by-side grid on `lg` (rejected to avoid cramped odds on narrow charts first pass; easy to add later).

## Assumptions
- `section-odds` id stays the same so in-page nav and deep links still work.

## Failure Modes
- Very long moneyline text on narrow screens may wrap; `min-w-0` on columns mitigates overflow.

## How to Test (commands + steps)
1. Open a game matchup page (e.g. `/betting/games/[id]`).
2. Scroll to **Odds & lines** in the sticky nav — one card shows odds then charts.
3. Toggle Spread / Total tabs; charts render as before.

## Next Steps
