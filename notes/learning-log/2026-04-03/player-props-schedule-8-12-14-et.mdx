## Context
Player props controller schedule in Terraform was updated to run three times per Eastern day.

## Goal
- **Goal:** Align `player_props_schedule_crons` with 8 AM, 12 PM, and 2 PM Eastern daily.
- **Inputs:** Existing `infra/terraform.tfvars` player props block; EventBridge Scheduler uses `player_props_schedule_timezone` (`America/New_York`).
- **Output/Done:** Three cron expressions in tfvars; deploy via `terraform apply` when ready.

## Files Changed
- `infra/terraform.tfvars`
- `notes/learning-log/2026-04-03/player-props-schedule-8-12-14-et.mdx`

## Implementation Notes (key logic explained)
- `aws_scheduler_schedule.player_props_crons` in `infra/lambda.tf` creates **one schedule per** `player_props_schedule_crons` entry, all with `schedule_expression_timezone = America/New_York`.
- AWS cron format: `cron(minutes hours day-of-month month day-of-week year)`; `cron(0 12 ? * * *)` is **noon** ET, not midnight.

## Key Decisions (and alternatives considered)
- **Decision:** Treat “12” in the request as **12:00 PM (noon)** ET, not 12:00 AM midnight, for a sensible props-ingestion cadence.
- **Alternative:** Use `cron(0 0 ? * * *)` for true midnight ET; say the word if you want that instead of or in addition to noon.

## Assumptions
- “12 am” in the user message was phrasing for **noon**; if you meant midnight, hour should be `0` instead of `12`.

## Failure Modes
- Changes **do not** hit AWS until `terraform apply` in `infra`.
- Wrong interpretation of “12” → schedule at noon vs midnight.
